The Story of Apple Inc.

Founding and Early Years

Apple Inc. was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. The company’s first product, the Apple I, was a single-board computer sold as a kit.

Apple quickly gained attention with the launch of the Apple II in 1977, which was one of the first successful mass-market personal computers.

Innovation and Expansion

Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, Apple introduced several groundbreaking products including the Macintosh in 1984, known for its graphical user interface and iconic Super Bowl ad.

In 2007, Apple revolutionized the mobile phone industry with the iPhone, and in 2010 it introduced the iPad, transforming the tablet market.

  • Founded: April 1, 1976
  • Key Products: Apple I, Macintosh, iPhone, iPad
  • CEO Timeline: Steve Jobs → Tim Cook (2011–present)
  • Known For: Innovation, sleek design, and ecosystem integration
